Below is a **Future Reality Tree** for the Second Renaissance group, built from the CRT and CRDs.

In LTP, the Future Reality Tree asks: **If we introduce these injections, will they logically create the desired effects and eliminate the undesirable effects?** Dettmer describes an **injection** as a new condition or action that does not yet exist in current reality and must be made to happen for the desired future to unfold. He also cautions that injections are not yet “solutions”; they become solutions only after they are tested, planned, and implemented.

---

# **Future Reality Tree: Second Renaissance Group**

## **Desired future condition**

**The group reliably increases Second Renaissance throughput: durable adoption of wiser views, values, practices, and social forms by people, communities, pockets, sanghas, and institutions.**

This aligns with the Second Renaissance papers’ core claim that the transition requires shifts in ways of **being, thinking, feeling, and acting**, and must include pragmatic embodiment, not only ideas.

---

# **Core injections** **Injection 0 — Establish tree stewardship and revision protocol**

# The group creates a lightweight but explicit process for maintaining the LTP trees as living documents.

# It includes:

* # version history

* # change proposals

* # objection logging

* # evidence standards

* # decision rules

* # unresolved minority reports

* # review cadence

* # named but accountable stewards

* # criteria for “good enough for provisional use”

## **Injection 1 — Define throughput operationally**

The group defines throughput as:  
verified durable adoption of Second Renaissance-aligned practices,  
capacities, social forms, or institutional patterns.

This replaces vague progress with a clear operational standard.

---

## **Injection 2 — Build a staged conversion architecture**

The group creates a pathway:  
reach → meaningful contact → first practice → cohort →  
sustained practice → contribution → facilitation →  
pocket / sangha formation → replication.

This directly addresses the CRT’s likely system constraint: weak conversion from resonance into durable practice.

---

## **Injection 3 — Establish layered participation**

The group defines different layers of participation:  
movement, sangha, cohort, pocket, steward/facilitator.  
Each layer has clear commitments, rights, responsibilities, and next steps.

This dissolves the inclusivity/coherence conflict by making the edge open and the center more committed.

---

## **Injection 4 — Install a practice-action-learning cadence**

Every initiative runs as a learning cycle:  
hypothesis → action → result → learning → decision.

This prevents drifting into activity without learning.

---

## **Injection 5 — Create a graduated stewardship pathway**

Members can move from participant to contributor to facilitator to steward  
through apprenticeship, standards, feedback, and review.

This reduces founder bottleneck and increases capacity.

---

## **Injection 6 — Build dense pocket containers**

The group deliberately creates environments with enough shared practice,  
interaction density, commitment, and support for new ways of life to stabilize.

The “How” paper says a pocket requires a majority of members to share commitment to the new way of life, and enough interaction with other pocket members to sustain that way of life against mainstream pressure.

---

## **Injection 7 — Create a replication library**

The group documents tested practices, rituals, governance patterns,  
onboarding scripts, curricula, and pocket-formation guides  
so other groups can adopt them without depending on the founders.

---

## **Injection 8 — Monitor constraints monthly**

The group holds a monthly throughput and constraint review:  
What increased durable adoption?  
Where is flow stuck?  
What is the current constraint?  
What will we change next?

# **Negative branches and trims**

Dettmer recommends checking an FRT for **negative branches**: possible undesirable outcomes caused by the injections themselves. He says these should be identified after the basic FRT is built, then “trimmed” with additional injections.

## **Negative Branch 1 — Throughput metrics become reductive**

I1: Throughput is clearly defined.  
        ↓  
The group starts measuring adoption-events.  
        ↓  
People optimize for counted adoption-events.  
        ↓  
Subtle inner transformation, grief, wisdom, and relationship quality get neglected.  
        ↓  
UDE-NB1:  
The group becomes managerial and loses soul.

### **Trim injection**

T1:  
Use mixed-method measurement:  
quantitative adoption metrics \+ qualitative stories \+  
peer discernment \+ periodic wisdom review.

---

## **Negative Branch 2 — Conversion architecture becomes manipulative**

I2: Staged conversion architecture exists.  
        ↓  
The group designs pathways from interest to commitment.  
        ↓  
People feel managed, recruited, or funneled.  
        ↓  
Trust decreases.  
        ↓  
UDE-NB2:  
The pathway feels extractive or cult-like.

### **Trim injection**

T2:  
Make the pathway transparent, consent-based, and exit-friendly.  
Every stage includes informed choice, no pressure, and clear rights.

---

## **Negative Branch 3 — Layered participation creates hierarchy and status games**

I3: Layered participation exists.  
        ↓  
Some roles appear more advanced or prestigious.  
        ↓  
People attach identity and status to layers.  
        ↓  
Competition, exclusion, or spiritual bypassing emerges.  
        ↓  
UDE-NB3:  
The group reproduces hierarchy under new language.

### **Trim injection**

T3:  
Define layers as responsibility levels, not worth levels.  
Rotate service roles, publish role criteria, and include status-shadow work.

---

## **Negative Branch 4 — Pockets become insular**

I6: Dense pocket containers exist.  
        ↓  
Members spend more time with aligned people.  
        ↓  
The pocket becomes self-referential.  
        ↓  
It loses contact with ordinary life and wider society.  
        ↓  
UDE-NB4:  
Pockets become enclaves rather than seeds of cultural transition.

### **Trim injection**

T4:  
Require each pocket to maintain outward-facing service,  
cultural contribution, and relationship with the wider sangha.

---

## **Negative Branch 5 — Replication spreads shallow copies**

I7: Replication library exists.  
        ↓  
Other groups copy forms without depth.  
        ↓  
Practices are imitated without context, training, or maturity.  
        ↓  
The work becomes diluted.  
        ↓  
UDE-NB5:  
Replication scales the shell, not the substance.

### **Trim injection**

T5:  
Use maturity ratings:  
prototype → tested → stable → teachable → replicable.  
Only “teachable” and “replicable” patterns are actively distributed.

# **Bottom line**

The Future Reality Tree says the group should introduce **structure in service of transformation**:

clear throughput  
\+ conversion pathway  
\+ layered participation  
\+ disciplined learning  
\+ stewardship development  
\+ dense pockets  
\+ replication library  
\+ monthly constraint review  
\= increased durable adoption

But the negative branches warn that the same structure can become reductive, manipulative, hierarchical, insular, or shallow unless it is balanced by transparency, consent, qualitative discernment, humility, outward service, and maturity standards.
