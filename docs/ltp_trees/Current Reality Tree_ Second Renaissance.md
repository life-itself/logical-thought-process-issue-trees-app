Below is a **provisional Current Reality Tree** for a group trying to make progress toward the Second Renaissance goal.

In LTP terms, a **Current Reality Tree** depicts the unfavorable part of the present system by connecting visible **Undesirable Effects** to deeper **root causes** through cause-and-effect logic. Dettmer says the CRT is used to articulate UDEs, relate them to root causes, identify the few critical root causes producing many of the UDEs, and isolate the system constraints that must be addressed for maximum improvement.

## **System under analysis**

**System:** A Second Renaissance group / community / initiative.

**Goal from the goal tree:**  
Co-initiate a Second Renaissance: a conscious transformation of foundational views, values, practices, and institutions toward a wiser, weller, regenerative civilization.

**Throughput definition:**  
The rate at which the group produces **durable adoption** of wiser views, values, practices, and social forms by people, groups, and institutions.

These are the visible symptoms that show the system is not yet producing enough throughput.

# **Critical Root Causes**

I would start with **five candidate Critical Root Causes**.

Dettmer cautions against trying to find one vague “core problem.” Instead, he recommends identifying a few **critical root causes** that are actionable and within the decision maker’s sphere of influence.

**CRC 0 — The group lacks a governance process for validating and updating its strategic maps.**

## **CRC 1 — The group lacks a clear operational definition of throughput.**

Because throughput is unclear, the group cannot reliably distinguish **real transformation** from **activity**.

## **CRC 2 — There is no explicit pathway from interest to practice to contribution to replication.**

People can encounter the ideas, but there is no well-designed conversion system that helps them move from inspiration into durable participation.

## **CRC 3 — The work is too cognitively framed and not sufficiently embodied in repeated practice.**

The Second Renaissance papers emphasize that this transition requires shifts in ways of **being, thinking, feeling, and acting**, and that pragmatic ways to embody and enact new ideas are as important as the ideas themselves.

## **CRC 4 — The group has not yet created sufficiently dense containers for pocket formation.**

The “How” paper says pockets require shared commitment, consistent practice, and enough interaction density to make a new way of life sustainable against the pressure of the mainstream.

## **CRC 5 — The group lacks a disciplined learning and constraint-management rhythm.**

Without a regular review of UDEs, constraints, experiments, and throughput, the system keeps improving locally rather than increasing the whole-system rate of transformation.

This is probably the main system trap.

# **Likely system constraint**

The current constraint is probably not “lack of awareness.”

It is more likely:

> **The group lacks a reliable conversion architecture that turns interest into sustained practice, contribution, pocket formation, and replication.**

That one constraint connects many UDEs:

Weak conversion architecture  
        ↓  
weak onboarding  
        ↓  
weak sustained practice  
        ↓  
few contributors  
        ↓  
few facilitators  
        ↓  
few pockets  
        ↓  
weak replication  
        ↓  
low throughput

The “How” paper’s transition model makes this especially important: paradigmatic change moves through **pockets**, **sangha**, and **movement**, and these levels are mutually dependent. Pockets trial new ways of life; sangha nourishes pockets; movement gives visibility, shared identity, and broader participation.

---

# **What to validate next**

A real CRT should be tested against lived evidence. The group should ask:

1. **Are these really our UDEs?**  
   Which symptoms are observable?  
2. **Which UDEs are worst relative to the goal tree?**  
   Low attendance may not matter; low durable adoption does.  
3. **Do the causal arrows hold?**  
   For each arrow, ask: “If the cause exists, does the effect necessarily or probably follow?”  
4. **What is missing?**  
   Some branches may need added causes such as money, governance, conflict skill, narrative clarity, or social trust.  
5. **Which root causes are within our influence?**  
   A CRT is useful only if it points to actionable leverage.

---

# **Working conclusion**

The CRT suggests that the group should not primarily solve for “more reach,” “more content,” or “more events.”

It should first solve for:

> **A clear throughput definition plus a repeatable pathway that converts resonance into sustained practice, contribution, pocket formation, and replication.**

That is the most promising intervention point for increasing progress toward the Second Renaissance.
