Below are **Evaporating Clouds / Conflict Resolution Diagrams** for the Current Reality Tree we built.

In Dettmer’s LTP, the Conflict Resolution Diagram has one **objective**, two **necessary requirements**, two **conflicting prerequisites**, the **assumptions** that hold the conflict in place, and one or more **injections** that dissolve the conflict without simple compromise. Dettmer emphasizes that requirements themselves usually are not in conflict; the conflict is usually between the prerequisites people choose in order to satisfy those requirements.

---

### **CRD 0 — Strategic Coherence vs. Distributed Legitimacy**

# Objective:  Use the LTP trees to guide effective Second Renaissance R\&D.

# Requirement A:  Maintain strategic coherence, clarity, and momentum.

# Requirement B:  Ensure distributed legitimacy, plural intelligence, and revisability.

# Prerequisite A:  Let the tree creator or a small strategy group maintain the trees decisively.

# Prerequisite B:  Let the wider group review, challenge, and revise the trees collectively.

# Conflict:  Centralized stewardship seems efficient but risks illegitimacy and blind spots.  Collective revision seems legitimate but risks endless discussion, diffusion, and paralysis.

# Hidden assumption:  The group must choose between author-controlled coherence and open-ended collective editing.

# Injection:  Create a bounded tree-governance protocol: distributed input, explicit criteria, accountable stewards, transparent decision rules, and time-bounded revision cycles.

# New operating rule:  The trees are neither personal property nor open-edit chaos. They are stewarded commons.

# That phrase — stewarded commons — feels especially apt for this group.

# **CRD 1 — Reach vs. Depth**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Increase Second Renaissance throughput:  
durable adoption of wiser views, values, practices, and social forms.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Reach enough people to create cultural momentum.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Deepen enough people to create real embodied transformation.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Prioritize broad public-facing activity:  
content, events, media, talks, partnerships, growth.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Prioritize deep practice containers:  
cohorts, retreats, facilitation, relational work, pocket formation.

CONFLICT:  
We cannot prioritize broad reach and deep containers with the same limited attention,  
time, money, and leadership capacity.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
To reach enough people, we must put most resources into public activity.

B → PB:  
To deepen people, we must put most resources into intensive containers.

PA ↔ PB:  
Broad activity and deep containers compete for the same scarce organizer capacity.

Hidden assumption:  
Reach and depth are separate workstreams rather than stages in one conversion system.

## **Injection**

Build a staged conversion architecture:  
public reach → first practice → cohort → contribution → facilitation → pocket/sangha formation.

## **Dissolved logic**

The group does **not** choose “reach” or “depth.” It designs reach specifically to feed depth, and depth specifically to generate future reach through practitioners, facilitators, pockets, and replication.

Public activity is successful only if it converts people into practice.  
Deep practice is successful only if it creates people capable of transmitting the work.

## **New operating rule**

No major public-facing activity without a next-step pathway.  
No deep container without a replication or contribution pathway.

This fits the Second Renaissance theory of change: pockets, sangha, and movement are interdependent; pockets trial new ways of life, sangha nourishes them, and movement spreads visibility, participation, and shared narrative.

# **CRD 2 — Inclusivity vs. Coherence**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Build a pluralistic Second Renaissance movement capable of real cultural transition.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Remain open, inclusive, non-totalizing, and accessible.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Maintain enough shared commitment, practice, and coherence to form real pockets.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Keep boundaries loose and language broad so many people can belong.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Set clear commitments, norms, practices, and standards for participation.

CONFLICT:  
Loose boundaries appear to undermine coherence.  
Clear commitments appear to undermine openness.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
If we set strong commitments, people will experience the group as exclusive or dogmatic.

B → PB:  
If we do not set strong commitments, the group will never become a coherent culture.

PA ↔ PB:  
A group must choose between being open and being demanding.

Hidden assumption:  
Everyone must participate at the same level of commitment.

## **Injection**

Create layered participation:  
movement → sangha → practice cohort → pocket → steward/facilitator.  
Each layer has different commitments, rights, responsibilities, and expectations.

## **Dissolved logic**

The group can be **open at the edge** and **coherent at the center**.

Movement layer: low-barrier participation.  
Sangha layer: recurring community of practice.  
Pocket layer: high-commitment shared life/practice container.  
Steward layer: explicit responsibilities and standards.

## **New operating rule**

Do not lower pocket standards to make the movement inclusive.  
Do not make movement participation so demanding that only pocket-level members can join.

The “How” paper defines pockets as environments where most members share commitment to the new way of life and where most interactions occur with others in the pocket; sangha is the wider community of practice that supports the pocket while remaining more embedded in mainstream life.

# **CRD 3 — Founder Control vs. Distributed Leadership**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Increase throughput without exhausting the core team.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Protect quality, coherence, trust, and integrity of the work.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Distribute responsibility so the work can grow beyond the founders.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Founders/core organizers must retain control over decisions, facilitation, narrative, and standards.

PREREQUISITE B:  
New contributors must be empowered to lead, adapt, and make decisions.

CONFLICT:  
Founder control limits distributed leadership.  
Distributed leadership appears to risk quality and coherence.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
Quality will fall if founders do not control important work.

B → PB:  
The system cannot scale unless new people lead.

PA ↔ PB:  
Control and empowerment are mutually exclusive.

Hidden assumption:  
Leadership can only be transferred by either full control or full delegation.

## **Injection**

Create a graduated stewardship pathway:  
observe → assist → co-facilitate → facilitate with review → steward independently → train others.

## **Dissolved logic**

Quality is protected through **apprenticeship, standards, feedback, and review**, not permanent founder control.

Founders shift from doing the work to building the conditions under which others can do the work well.

## **New operating rule**

Every recurring function must have:  
1\. an owner,  
2\. an apprentice,  
3\. explicit standards,  
4\. feedback loops,  
5\. a succession path.

This is directly related to the CRT’s likely constraint: weak conversion from resonance into sustained practice, contribution, facilitation, pocket formation, and replication.

# **CRD 4 — Reflection vs. Action**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Make wise progress toward cultural transformation.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Act in the world, build things, and respond to urgent crises.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Slow down enough for wisdom, inner development, learning, and right relationship.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Move quickly: launch programs, publish, organize, partner, intervene.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Slow down: practice, reflect, metabolize, build trust, sense deeply.

CONFLICT:  
Moving quickly seems to crowd out depth.  
Slowing down seems to delay needed action.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
Urgent times require rapid outward action.

B → PB:  
Wise action requires slowness, reflection, and inner development.

PA ↔ PB:  
Speed and wisdom are incompatible.

Hidden assumption:  
Reflection and action must happen in separate phases.

## **Injection**

Adopt a practice-action-learning cadence:  
small action → reflection → integration → next action.

## **Dissolved logic**

The group does not choose between contemplation and action. It makes **action smaller, faster, and more learnable**, while making reflection disciplined and consequential.

Act in short cycles.  
Reflect immediately.  
Update the model.  
Act again.

## **New operating rule**

Every initiative is an experiment with:  
hypothesis, action, result, learning, decision.

The Second Renaissance materials emphasize that the transition requires shifts in ways of being, thinking, feeling, and acting, and that pragmatic embodiment matters as much as ideas.

# **CRD 5 — Accessibility vs. Transformational Challenge**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Help many people enter real Second Renaissance practice.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Make the work accessible to ordinary people in ordinary life.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Preserve the depth and challenge required for real transformation.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Simplify language, reduce demands, and make participation easy.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Use demanding practices, complex ideas, and strong commitments.

CONFLICT:  
Making the work easy appears to dilute it.  
Keeping the work deep appears to make it inaccessible.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
People will not join if the work is too demanding or abstract.

B → PB:  
The work will not transform people if it is not demanding and deep.

PA ↔ PB:  
Accessible means shallow; deep means inaccessible.

Hidden assumption:  
All material must be delivered at the same depth to all participants.

## **Injection**

Create progressive depth:  
plain-language entry points,  
guided beginner practices,  
intermediate cohorts,  
advanced pocket/stewardship commitments.

## **Dissolved logic**

Depth becomes a **pathway**, not a barrier.

The doorway is simple.  
The path is deep.  
The commitments increase as capacity and trust increase.

## **New operating rule**

Translate every core idea into:  
1\. a one-sentence version,  
2\. a story,  
3\. a practice,  
4\. a deeper study path,  
5\. a community application.

This also respects the whitepaper’s caution that “Second Renaissance” is an imperfect and culturally situated frame, not a totalizing term for everyone.

# **CRD 6 — Movement Growth vs. Pocket Integrity**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Support a Second Renaissance transition from pockets to sangha to movement.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Scale visibility, participation, and mainstream influence.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Protect the integrity, maturity, and lived coherence of pockets.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Scale quickly through media, partnerships, public narratives, and institutional influence.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Delay scaling until pockets are mature, grounded, and tested.

CONFLICT:  
Scaling quickly risks shallow or distorted adoption.  
Waiting for maturity risks missing the historical window.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
Without scale, the work remains marginal and irrelevant to civilizational crisis.

B → PB:  
Without mature pockets, scaling reproduces the old paradigm in new language.

PA ↔ PB:  
Scale and integrity must be traded off.

Hidden assumption:  
Scaling means broadcasting the whole model before it is ready.

## **Injection**

Use differentiated scaling:  
scale the invitation and narrative early;  
scale practices only after testing;  
scale pocket models only after demonstrated durability;  
scale stewardship only through apprenticeship.

## **Dissolved logic**

The group can scale different things at different maturity levels.

Narrative can travel early.  
Practices travel after validation.  
Governance models travel after stress testing.  
Pocket replication travels after durability.  
Stewardship travels through lineage and training.

## **New operating rule**

Each pattern receives a maturity rating before replication:  
prototype → tested → stable → teachable → replicable.

The “How” paper warns that before trying to drive social change at scale, pockets require deep care, grounding, wisdom, and bottom-up experience; it also says movement-building is needed from an early stage to maintain larger purpose.

# **CRD 7 — Ideological Clarity vs. Non-Dogmatism**

## **Conflict**

OBJECTIVE:  
Create shared orientation without becoming rigid or sectarian.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Have enough shared language, worldview, values, and direction to coordinate.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Remain open, humble, adaptive, and non-dogmatic.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Define the paradigm clearly and teach it with confidence.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Avoid fixed doctrine and keep multiple frames in play.

CONFLICT:  
Clear doctrine appears to create dogmatism.  
Plural frames appear to create confusion.

## **Assumptions holding the conflict**

A → PA:  
Coordination requires a clear shared worldview.

B → PB:  
Wisdom requires humility and openness to revision.

PA ↔ PB:  
A group cannot be both clear and non-dogmatic.

Hidden assumption:  
Clarity must take the form of fixed belief rather than provisional orientation.

## **Injection**

Use “strong opinions, lightly held” doctrine:  
clear provisional principles,  
explicit uncertainty,  
regular revision,  
multiple valid framings,  
practice-based verification.

## **Dissolved logic**

The group distinguishes **orientation** from **orthodoxy**.

We can say clearly what we are currently trying,  
why we believe it matters,  
what evidence would change our mind,  
and how others can adapt it responsibly.

## **New operating rule**

Every principle is paired with:  
1\. its purpose,  
2\. its risks,  
3\. its shadow,  
4\. its revision process.

This is consistent with the Second Renaissance emphasis on meta-paradigmatic awareness: paradigms can evolve, and no single frame should assume it “has the answer.”

# **Consolidated injections**

Across the diagrams, the main injections are:

1\. Staged conversion architecture:  
   reach → practice → cohort → contribution → facilitation → pocket/sangha → replication.

2\. Layered participation:  
   movement → sangha → cohort → pocket → steward.

3\. Graduated stewardship:  
   observe → assist → co-facilitate → lead with review → steward → train others.

4\. Practice-action-learning cadence:  
   hypothesis → action → result → learning → decision.

5\. Progressive depth:  
   simple entry → guided practice → deeper study → commitment → leadership.

6\. Differentiated scaling:  
   scale narrative, practices, governance, pocket models, and stewardship at different maturity levels.

7\. Provisional doctrine:  
   clear principles without fixed ideology.

## **Core conflict beneath the whole CRT**

The deepest conflict may be this:

OBJECTIVE:  
Increase durable cultural transformation.

REQUIREMENT A:  
Become visible enough to attract energy, people, money, and legitimacy.

REQUIREMENT B:  
Become embodied enough to actually represent the new paradigm.

PREREQUISITE A:  
Act like a movement now.

PREREQUISITE B:  
Act like a monastery/laboratory now.

CONFLICT:  
Movement logic pulls toward scale, speed, message, and accessibility.  
Monastery/laboratory logic pulls toward depth, slowness, practice, and rigor.

## **Core injection**

Become a movement with monasteries/laboratories at its center:  
a broad public invitation organized around deep, protected, practice-based pockets.

That gives the group a way to pursue scale **through** depth rather than instead of depth.
